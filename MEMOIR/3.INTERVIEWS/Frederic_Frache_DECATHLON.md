### Interview Questions:

1. Decathlon works in the sports sector, which relates to very physical activities. In your view, what are the differences between developing a product and its materials in a more traditional way and doing so digitally?

2. In your opinion, what is lost or transformed in the transition from one reality to another — that is, in the translation from the physical to the digital?

3. Regarding the sense of touch: we always work with physical objects and experiences. Is this something you miss, or that sometimes creates difficulties? Perhaps you have an example of a project where this question came up?

4. In terms of workflows, how do you approach the digitization of materials? Do you always start from a physical sample?

5. What are the biggest challenges you encounter in this process?

6. At Decathlon, what is the main goal behind creating these digital materials? For what purposes have they been used so far?

7. Do these “digital twins” — I’m not sure if you use that term — ever get presented directly to the public, or are they used only for internal processes?

    - If yes: for what purpose were they made accessible to the public, and how did people react?
    - If not: in what situation do you think this could be done? What kind of feedback would you like to receive from users?

8. From a digital — or even hybrid (digital + physical) — perspective, how do you imagine or wish people would experience Decathlon 50 years from now?
